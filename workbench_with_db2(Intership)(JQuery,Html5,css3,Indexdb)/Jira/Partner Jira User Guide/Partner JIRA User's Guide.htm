Chapter 1: Overview

Partner JIRA is GTECH’s customer service portal.  Partner JIRA provides web-access to real-time information about a customer’s existing enhancement and maintenance requests, as well as the status of customer testing issues.  This information is available without intervention from GTECH and allows GTECH’s customers to monitor the status of the issues they’ve entered independently.  As Partner JIRA data is tied into GTECH’s internal issue tracking system, the information reflects the current status of any customer enhancement requests or reported defects.

Partner JIRA allows customers to enter their own requests, facilitating the existing communication mechanisms with GTECH.  Users can enter information independent from GTECH, providing details about suspected problems or enhancements.  Allowing customers to enter their own requests will lessen the impact of potential problems with other communication mechanisms.  Entries into Partner JIRA can also include attachments and comments to help completely describe the request.

Partner JIRA provides GTECH the ability to respond to customer requests directly through a common mechanism.  GTECH can provide feedback on those requests through Partner JIRA allowing customers to receive GTECH feedback on potential enhancements through a common and consistent interface. 

Partner JIRA provides a level of collaboration and visibility that will enhance and extend the exceptional level of support to which GTECH customers are accustomed.

For any issues and/or questions concerning Partner JIRA, please contact your GTECH Service/Project Lead.

Understanding Issue Workflow

After you create the issue, you will be brought to this page.  Note the series of buttons highlighted in red. 

 

You can assign an issue to another user or yourself in any status.

Prior to submission of your issue to GTECH, you can edit the issue as many times as you’d like – add attachments, change the description, increase or decrease the severity level, etc.  Click Edit and an Edit box will open where you can make your changes.

Update the issue using the Edit button at the bottom of the screen and the change will be reflected in your issue.

 

You can also Cancel the issue prior to submission.   Enhancement requests will move to Closed, Won’t Implement status.   TIR-Live and CAT issues will move to Closed, Not A Problem status.

 

When all your changes have been made, click Submit to GTECH.  A corresponding issue will be created in the GTECH system.

 

From this point forward, all communication about the issue will occur in the Comments section.  Click Submit Comment to GTECH to write a comment, submit a question, or respond to a question posed by GTECH regarding the issue.   Comments from GTECH will also appear in the same location as your comments.

When there a status update, your issue will reflect the change.  The issue can either be deferred or assigned to a developer.  If the issue is deferred it can later be reopened and then progress will begin. 

 

The next status change you should expect will be when the issue is Ready for Customer Test:

 

When the issue has been released to you, you can begin Customer Testing to verify that the original issue on your system has been resolved.  You have the option to either Fail or Pass CAT:

If you select Fail CAT, you are required to enter a comment with details and then the issue is reopened:

 

Otherwise, if you Pass CAT, the issue is now ready for production.

GTECH will update the status to Closed when they make the release.

* Note that TIR – Live uses the same workflow as TIR – Customer Test, which has been shown here.

Understanding Enhancement Request Workflow

As with Customer Test and TIR-Live issues, from this point forward, all communication about the issue will occur in the Comments section.  Click Submit Comment to GTECH to write a comment, submit a question, or respond to a question posed by GTECH regarding the issue.   Comments from GTECH will also appear in the same location as your comments.

 

Submit the Request to GTECH when ready.

And the issue will transition to the Open status.

GTECH may defer the issue or close it without resolving after discussing with you, the customer.  Once deferred, there remains the option that the request can be reopened again in the future.  The request may also not be approved.  If that is the case, you will see this status change:

Once a developer has been assigned, progress will begin.

 

 

Unless the issue is deferred or closed, the next status change you will see is when the request is Ready for Customer Test.

Once the request is released, you can begin your testing.  You can either Pass CAT or Fail CAT.  Fail CAT can be found by clicking the Workflow button.

  If you Fail CAT, you’ll be required to enter details in the comment box.  Otherwise, if you Pass CAT, the issue is now Ready for Production.

GTECH will update the status to Closed, Passed Verification when they make the release.
